The International Olive Oil Association divides olive oil into two main categories and five levels: Virgin Olive Oil and Lampante Olive Oil or Refined Olive Oil.
Extra virgin olive oil, also known as natural olive oil, is the oil obtained by mechanically cold pressing fresh olive fruits and removing impurities through filtration and other treatments.
According to the different acidity levels, it can be divided into three categories:
Extra Virgin Olive Oil: The highest grade and highest quality olive oil, a pure natural product.
Excellent taste, with a delicate and pleasant plant fragrance, acidity not exceeding 1%.
Fine Virgin Olive Oil: Slightly higher acidity, but not exceeding 2%, with a pure and fragrant taste.
Ordinary Virgin Olive Oil: The taste and flavor are acceptable, with an acidity not exceeding 3.3%.
Refined olive oil refers to the olive oil obtained after refining virgin olive oil with an acidity of more than 3.3%, or becoming "secondary oil".
Refined olive oil can be divided into two levels:
Ordinary Olive Oil: A blend of refined olive oil and a certain proportion of virgin olive oil to balance the taste and color, with an acidity of less than 1.5%, presenting a transparent light golden yellow color.
Refined Olive-Pomace Oil: It is an olive oil obtained by extracting from pomace through the solvent method and refining.
After seeing the grade classification, you should have noticed that the acidity of olive oil is a very important indicator.
You should know that olives are like grapes used for making wine; olives from different regions and different varieties will produce olive oil of different standards.
The best growing area for olives:
The Mediterranean coast has the most suitable soil and climatic conditions for the growth of olive oil, such as temperature, sunlight, air humidity, and precipitation.
Except for the Mediterranean coast, other areas can only be sporadically planted in suitable habitats.
Main production areas and origins (Mediterranean):
Italy, Spain, Greece, Turkey, Portugal, Morocco, Syria, Israel, Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt, Pakistan, Lebanon, and so on, as well as countries with similar climates in the southern latitudes, such as Argentina, Peru, South Africa, and so on.
Olive harvesting month:
From March to June every year, it is the time for olive trees to bloom and bear fruit. From July to October, the fruits begin to develop and mature, transitioning from green to red, purple, and finally black.
November to February is the fruit picking season (the freshness of olive oil is very important).
Nutritional components of olive oil:
Olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fatty acids, including oleic acid, linoleic acid, and linolenic acid, as well as vitamins A, B, D, E, K, and antioxidants.
Olive oil is considered to be the most suitable oil for human nutrition among all the fats discovered so far.

Overdue registrants who have not provided continuing education proof should provide proof of no less than 30 hours of continuing education in the past year. For re-registration, proof of no less than 120 hours of continuing education in the past 4 years should be provided. For re-registration, proof of no less than 30 hours of continuing education per full year should be provided.
Cost Engineer Continuing Education Fee Standard
Cost engineers participating in online continuing education must pay the learning fees to the respective management organizations when registering. The general standard is: the cost of 30 compulsory class hours is 300 yuan/person/year (including unified continuing education materials).
The cost of a 30-hour elective course is 300 yuan per person per year.
The cost of 30 compulsory class hours and 30 elective class hours is 600 yuan/person/year (including unified continuing education materials).
(1) Participate in the online continuing education learning for registered cost engineers organized by the China Pricing Association or provincial and departmental management agencies, and calculate the study hours according to the time recorded in the online learning courseware;
(2) Participating in centralized face-to-face training for registered cost engineers organized by the China Pricing Association or provincial and departmental management agencies, as well as various types of training courses, seminars, etc., can be recognized as 4 credit hours for every half day;
(3) Participate in the China Pricing Association, provincial and departmental cost engineers, and cost staff continuing education lectures, with 10 study hours recognized for every half day;
(IV) Participate in the research of provincial-level topics in the China Price Association, industry standard formulation, and textbook compilation; each item is recognized as 30 academic hours per year.
(6) Participating in the compilation of teaching materials for the national cost engineer qualification examination, as well as setting, reviewing, and grading questions, can be recognized as 30 study hours per year;
(7) Engineering cost-related professional papers and works that are signed by individuals and published publicly (based on the official publication number).

(IX) Participation in public training organized by the China Engineering Cost Association, provincial and departmental management agencies, and provincial cost associations approved or authorized engineering cost consulting enterprises (excluding general management and skill training for enterprises) can be recognized as 4 credit hours per half day, with a maximum of 30 credit hours recognized per year.

Educational Thoughts: How to Make College Exams Fair for Both "Top Students" and "Poor Students" - Xinhua News Agency
Three students from Lanzhou Jiaotong University wrote a letter to the school leaders, reflecting their confusion about university exams, and proposed to increase the difficulty of exams, change the types of exam questions, and let exams truly reflect the actual level of students.
Indeed, for a long time, university exams have been a "hot topic".
In the university campus, students come from all corners of the country, with varying levels of ability, and different levels of education, resulting in a myriad of learning situations.
For a group with such diverse characteristics, it is inevitable that there will be different feelings when being assessed.
Of course, if the exam questions are of a single type, have a high repetition rate, and are too simple, it will inevitably lead to a completely different feeling.
Students who don't study hard may be able to pass by cramming before the exam. If they try other methods, they might achieve twice the results with half the effort.
As a result, the "poor students" can pass and even get high scores without putting in much effort, while the "top students" who never miss classes and have good homework and notes don't score much higher in exams, creating a "strange situation".
In this view, the concerns raised by Lanzhou Jiaotong University students about issues in university exams are not unfounded, and it is believed that this situation is not unique to Lanzhou Jiaotong University.
From this perspective, we should give a thumbs up to these three students who wrote the letter directly.
In fact, people who have attended college are well aware that cramming for exams, trying various ways to "extract" exam questions from teachers, striving to narrow down the scope of the exam, and seeking "focused tutoring" before the exam are not uncommon phenomena. Even bringing cheat sheets to exams and asking teachers for leniency after the exam are not unheard of.
In the author's view, as a teaching quality assessment orientation, measuring the pass or fail of a university exam depends more on the teacher's comprehensive grasp of teaching and learning, as well as the scientific proposition based on this foundation.
Upon entering the undergraduate stage, majors begin to subdivide, and many specialized courses are mainly based on fundamental knowledge.
Without a doubt, whether one has a comprehensive grasp of the basic knowledge points of a subject is the content that university exams aim to assess.
The undergraduate knowledge mainly consists of basic theoretical foundations and common sense of the discipline. In terms of knowledge points, the room for change is not large, and it is basically fixed.
As a teacher said, "The basic knowledge of university mathematics learned 20 years ago is still being studied today, and it is still the focus."
However, the fact that the knowledge points remain basically unchanged does not mean that the exam question types are single and the test questions are repetitive.
In many universities, examination questions are usually formed by a question-setting group, and then the test papers are organized by senior teachers in the subject. The grading of the exams is also participated in by all the teachers responsible for the subject, which can to some extent overcome the problem of repeated questions.
To create a set of test questions that conform to the actual teaching and learning situation, the questions should be based on the overall situation of students' learning, with difficulty following a normal distribution. It is also necessary to comprehensively grasp the teaching situation, fully analyze the students' learning level, so that the examination questions can dynamically grasp the difficulty and question types, highlighting the examination of basic knowledge while taking into account the examination of abilities, and innovating the design of question types at the same time.
Exams are the baton of university education; the type of exams will determine the corresponding test preparation plans for students. Over time, this will affect the effectiveness of university education.
Regarding the suggestion letter from students, Lanzhou Jiaotong University attaches great importance to it and has deployed a rectification plan throughout the school.
Require each teaching unit to organize teachers to carry out research discussions and course final exams for self-inspection, formulate examination paper proposition and related systems, strengthen supervision and inspection of examination paper proposition and review links, refine the grading evaluation, and strengthen the comprehensive assessment of all aspects of course teaching.
Addressing the issues of single test question types and repeated questions in college examination administration from a systematic perspective is a topic that needs to be faced. Of course, corresponding systems need to be introduced, but the key lies in daily efforts. Teachers need to have a comprehensive grasp of the teaching situation, not only thoroughly understanding the teaching content, but also fully understanding the students' learning situation. By thoroughly understanding both aspects, teaching and learning will be better aligned, forming a positive interaction, and exams will be more in line with reality, allowing both "top students" and "struggling students" to find their place.

Qinghai Lake naked carp resources increase by 0.71 thousand tons this year - Xinhua News Agency
Xinhua News Agency, Xining, December 11 (Reporters Luo Xiaofei and Zhang Hongxiang) - Reporters learned from the Qinghai Provincial Department of Agriculture and Rural Affairs that the latest monitoring results show that the reserves of naked carp in Qinghai Lake increased by 0.71 thousand tons this year compared to last year, reaching 108.5 thousand tons. Compared with the initial stage of protection in 2002, the cumulative growth is more than 40 times.
Qinghai Lake naked carp, commonly known as "Huang fish", is a precious fish species unique to Qinghai Lake, playing a crucial role in maintaining the safety of the "water-fish-bird" ecological chain and biodiversity in the Qinghai Lake basin.
In the 1960s and 1970s, due to factors such as large-scale human hunting and the increasingly deteriorating environment of the naked carp's breeding waters, the number of naked carp in Qinghai Lake sharply decreased. In 2002, the reserve of naked carp resources in Qinghai Lake was only 2,592 tons.
In 2004, the Qinghai Lake naked carp was listed as an endangered species in the "China Species Red List".
Since 2003, Qinghai Province has implemented lake closure for fish breeding, prohibiting any unit, collective, and individual from fishing for naked carp in Qinghai Lake and the main rivers and tributaries in the lake area, and banning the sale of naked carp and its products.
At the same time, through artificial breeding and stocking, cleaning the main river channels entering Qinghai Lake, and designing scientifically reasonable "fish passages" and other measures, the protection efforts have been continuously increased, and the reserves of Qinghai Lake naked carp have been increasing year by year.
Yang Lin, deputy director of the Department of Agriculture and Rural Affairs of Qinghai Province, introduced that up to now, nearly 180 million artificially propagated and released naked carp have been implemented in Qinghai Lake, with a contribution rate of over 23% to the restoration of naked carp resources in Qinghai Lake.
The Sanjiangyuan region in Qinghai is an important ecological barrier and water conservation area in China and even Asia, while Qinghai Lake is a natural barrier and ecological function area that controls the eastward spread of desertification in the west.
In order to protect the ecological environment of the Sanjiangyuan area and Qinghai Lake waters, protect aquatic wildlife, and maintain the biodiversity of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau, Qinghai has successively issued fishing ban notices for the key waters of the Yangtze River, Yellow River, and Qinghai Lake since this year, establishing a comprehensive fishing ban management system for "one river, one river, one lake."

A person in charge of the Yunnan Provincial Department of Human Resources and Social Affairs introduced that currently, Yunnan has developed a total of 448,300 public welfare positions in rural areas, which are used to provide employment for poverty alleviation labor and other rural disadvantaged groups.
